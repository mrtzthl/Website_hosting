About Me

Physicist with a love for laboratories. Studies in Munich and San Diego, research in the lab, fun in figuring out how things work.

  • Education
    M.Sc. Physics, Technical University of Munich. Focus: Electrochemistry and computer-based physics.

§ 02

Things I've Printed

If you can measure it, you can design it. CAD in the evening, printer overnight, part in hand by morning.

  • Clock in train station style
    Inspired by German trains, my role model when it comes to punctuality, a wall clock.
  • Coffee grinder adapter
    The coffee grinder of choice is not compatible with the desired coffee machine? That can be easily fixed.
  • Many buttons, mounts, clamps
    Not every device that breaks something is immediately scrap. A bit of 3D printing and it works again - repairing is important, that's sustainable.
§ 03

Things I've Coded

Mostly Python, sometimes something new, always out of necessity. Vibe-coding has greatly expanded the boundaries of what's possible. Tools that solve smaller problems — not platforms.

  • Kindle Weather Dashboard
    Turns my old Kindle into something new. Every hour the current weather report, with a battery life that lasts forever.
  • Episode Tracker
    Collects favorite series in a list, automatically fetches new episodes via API and integrates them when possible. No streaming, just overview.
  • Recipe Extractor
    Script that scrapes cooking recipes from websites and converts them with AI into a uniform, ad-free Markdown format. Lands directly in your own note archive.
§ 04

Things I've Wired

Started with a soldering iron, ended up at PCBs. More sensors than the apartment really needs.

  • Daylight alarm clock
    A Pi Zero, MOSFETs, a speaker and some LED strips. Now getting up in winter is not as hard.
  • Zeppelin control
    A homemade zeppelin of course also needs propulsion and control, otherwise it would just be a better helium balloon. Servos, motors, antenna modules and an Arduino help with that. Fun fact: the DIY remote control can also open the garage door.
  • Weather satellite image receiver
    Small homemade antenna, USB-SDR receiver, a script. Catches weather images directly from orbit, completely without internet or subscription.
§ 05

Personal & Passionate

Beyond the workshop and code, there are things that drive me globally. Ancient stones, fast airplanes, and organizations that make our world a better place.

  • Exploring ancient ruins
    From Herculaneum to Delphi to My Son and Gyeongju — following the trail of history and understanding how past civilizations lived and built. Tsqaltubo in Georgia and the Genbaku Dome in Hiroshima also belong to this, ruins of the 20th century.
  • Concorde hunting in museums
    A technical masterpiece that was far ahead of its time. Every time I stand before a Concorde, I feel the pioneer spirit of aviation. Currently at 5/19
  • Organizations I support
    • Wikipedia
      Free knowledge for everyone. A world without Wikipedia would be a world without quick access to reliable information — essential for education and curiosity.
    • GFF – Gesellschaft für Freiheitsrechte
      Fights for digital fundamental rights in Germany. Without them, there would be less data protection, less transparency, and more government surveillance.
    • VSA – Verband der Stipendiaten der FNF
      Network for responsible leaders. Effective promotion of talent that advances society and science.
